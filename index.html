Square

Four equal sides, four right angles

Understanding Squares

All sides equal: s = s = s = s
All angles = 90°

Equal Sides

All four sides have exactly the same length (s = side length). Knowing one side tells you everything about the square's dimensions.

Right Angles

All four corners are perfect 90° angles. This makes squares ideal for construction, tiling, and design.

Symmetry

A square has 4 lines of symmetry and rotational symmetry. It looks identical after rotating 90°, 180°, or 270°.

A square is a regular quadrilateral with four equal sides and four 90° angles. Its symmetry makes it one of the most fundamental shapes in geometry.

Defining characteristics: All four sides have identical length (s), and all interior angles are right angles.

Key insight: Since all sides are equal, knowing one side length gives you complete information about the square's dimensions.

Area vs. Perimeter: What's the Difference?

Area measures the space inside the square. It tells you how much surface the square covers. For a square: Area = s²

Perimeter measures the distance around the square. It's the total length of all four sides added together. For a square: Perimeter = 4s

Area = s²
Measured in square units (cm², m²)

Perimeter = 4s
Measured in linear units (cm, m)

Quick Example: Square with side 5 cm

Area: How much space inside? A = 5² = 25 cm² (25 square centimeters)

Perimeter: How far around? P = 4 × 5 = 20 cm (20 centimeters)

Square Area Formula

The Formula

A = s²
Which means: A = s × s

When you multiply a side by itself, you get the total two-dimensional space enclosed by the square.

Why does this formula work?

Visual Understanding:
Imagine filling a square with 1×1 unit squares. If each side is 5 units long, you can fit 5 rows of 5 squares = 5 × 5 = 25 square units.

Mathematical Logic:
Area = length × width. For a square, length = width = s, so Area = s × s = s²

Worked Example

Question: Find the area of a square with side length 8 cm.

Solution:

Given: s = 8 cm
Formula: A = s²
A = (8)²
A = 8 × 8
A = 64 cm²

Answer: The area is 64 cm²
